Information and Troubleshooting


What is OLED?

OLED stands for Organic Light Emitting Diode. It is a popular form of display technology that is commonly found in the latest electronics that we use today such as smartphones, smart watches, tablets, laptops, televisions, and computer monitors. OLED displays are made up of thin layers of organic materials that emit light when an electric current is applied to them. Each OLED pixel can create its own light unlike LCD panels that require a backlight to illuminate all of the pixels together. Two big parts of OLED panels' allure are its ability to produce pure blacks and vibrant colors that other types of display technology such as LCD cannot recreate. Because of this and due to complex manufacturing processes, OLED panels are among the most expensive types of display technology that exist on the market.

What is vertical banding?

Vertical banding is dark or light vertical lines that appear on OLED panels that become more visible under dark, uniform backgrounds. It is a subcategory of a larger umbrella term called mura which is a Japanese word for unevenness. Vertical banding and mura in general is often an undesirable effect that distracts the viewer from what they are viewing. The reason why vertical banding appears more noticeable the darker the display is is that OLED pixels have a harder time precisely controlling the voltage to each pixel under these conditions, and thus, discrepancy in uniformity occurs. Vertical banding on OLED panels however is unfortunately naturally inherent due to how OLED technology is designed. During the manufacturing of these OLED Panels, it is very difficult to maintain accurate and uniform pixel brightness and becomes more noticeable the lower the light levels are. Because the level of visual distraction of vertical banding is purely subjective, it is up to the user to determine if the degree and locations of the vertical banding falls within an acceptable range for them and is satisfactory enough to generally ignore.

Why is vertical banding "vertical"?

The reason why vertical banding occurs as lines is because OLED panels are column-based. Each column is driven by a series of thin-film transistors (TFTs). TFTs control the voltage for all pixels in each column and gives them all the same voltage signal. If there is any variation in the voltage being sent to adjacent columns, vertical banding will appear as lines of varying shades on the display.

Why is vertical banding only a problem for OLED?

The truth is that LCD panels also suffer from vertical banding but it is much less noticeable due to the way that LCD panels are designed. Although LCD panels are also column-based and use TFTs, LCD panels use a backlight to illuminate all of the pixels at once on the display which creates a more uniform appearance. Additionally, because OLED panels are also capable of getting much darker than LCD, vertical banding also appears more noticeable as well.

How to reduce vertical banding?

There are a number of steps that you can take if you want to help reduce the amount of vertical banding that you see on your OLED panel.

1. Use a pixel refresher / cleaning tool.
Most OLED panels feature an pixel refresher tool that can be used to help reduce the appearance of vertical banding by individually adjusting the voltage on every pixel to help maintain the same light output and takes just a few minutes to perform. The pixel refresher / cleaning tool typically runs automatically after 4 hours of use but can be turned on manually through the settings menu of your OLED panel.

2. Play videos of varying colors and brightness.
Leaving your monitor on for a few hours and having it play continuous videos that frequently change color may help reduce the visibility of vertical banding by promoting uniform wear on the pixels.

3. Use your monitor normally and regularly for a few weeks or a few months
If step 2 didn't work, continue using your monitor normally and regularly for a few weeks or a few months as vertical banding may reduce over time as the pixels wear in a more uniform manner.

4. Contact OLED panel manufacturer.
If the vertical banding is still severe enough to be distracting, you can try contacting the manufacturer of your OLED panel to see if they have any suggestions or solutions for reducing vertical banding. If the panel is still under warranty, you may be able to get a replacement for your OLED panel that has less vertical banding.
